Caption Formulas
For Instagram carousels, LinkedIn posts, and any text-based content. Each formula includes the structure and a filled-in example you can model.

The PAS Framework (Problem → Agitate → Solve)

PROBLEM [1 line] → AGITATE [2-3 lines making it worse] → SOLVE [your framework/tip] → CTA
Your best employees are quietly disengaging. They're showing up on time, hitting deadlines, doing the bare minimum — and you think everything is fine. But here's what's actually happening: they stopped bringing ideas to meetings. They stopped volunteering for projects. They're updating their LinkedIn on their lunch break. By the time you notice, they already have an offer letter. Here are 3 things you can do THIS WEEK to re-engage your top performers: 1. Ask them what they'd change if they ran the department 2. Give them one project that scares them (growth = engagement) 3. Have a real conversation — not a review, a conversation Which one are you trying first? Drop it below.

The Listicle (numbered tips with micro-stories)

HOOK [bold claim or number] → LIST [3-7 tips with 1-2 line explanations] → WRAP [encouragement] → CTA
5 leadership habits that separate good managers from great ones: 1. They ask before they tell. Great leaders lead with questions, not answers. 2. They give credit publicly and give feedback privately. Every single time. 3. They protect their team's time like it's their own. No unnecessary meetings. 4. They hire people who challenge them. Not people who agree with them. 5. They admit when they're wrong. Out loud. In front of the team. Which one do you need to work on most? Save this and revisit it on Monday morning.

The LinkedIn Story Post

HOOK [1 line, punchy] → STORY [6-10 lines, personal] → LESSON [2-3 lines] → QUESTION [engagement driver]
I almost got fired from my first leadership role. Not because I wasn't working hard. I was putting in 14-hour days. The problem? I was doing everything myself. My team felt ignored. My boss felt I couldn't delegate. My results were good on paper but I was burning out everyone around me. One mentor pulled me aside and said something I'll never forget: "If you're the hardest worker in the room, you're the worst leader in the room." That changed everything. I started giving away my best work. I started trusting people who weren't perfect yet. I started measuring my success by my team's success. That's when everything took off. What's the best leadership advice someone ever gave you?

Part 9
Your 90-Day Execution Plan
Don't try to do everything at once. Here's the phased rollout so you build momentum without burning out.
DAYS 1-30: FOUNDATION

Fix, Clean, and Start

Week 1: Complete all emergency fixes (broken links, bios, brand names). Archive old Instagram posts. Set up consistent profile photos.
Week 2: Batch-film 8 short videos using the hook formulas and script templates above. Create your first 2 carousels. Set up a content folder on your phone.
Week 3: Start posting: 3 videos + 1 carousel + 1 LinkedIn text post. Pull 5 clips from your L3 Perspective archive.
Week 4: Post consistently all week. Review analytics. Double down on what got the most saves/shares. Engage with 10-15 accounts daily.
DAYS 31-60: MOMENTUM

Scale and Systematize

Week 5-6: Increase to 5 posts/week across platforms. Start the repurposing engine: 1 podcast episode → full week of content. Create your "Start Here" YouTube playlist.
Week 7-8: Launch a recurring series (e.g., "Monday Leadership Minute" or "AI Tool of the Week"). Series content creates habitual viewing which the algorithm rewards. Start collaborating: stitch/remix 2 other creators' content per week.
DAYS 61-90: ACCELERATION

Optimize and Convert

Week 9-10: Analyze your top 5 performing posts. Create 3 variations of each (same topic, different angle). This is the "outlier video" strategy — when you find something that works, make more of it.
Week 11-12: Launch your lead magnet funnel: bio link → free resource → email capture. Start emailing your list weekly with exclusive insights not on social. Set up your content batching system: 1 day per week to film and schedule everything.

The Non-Negotiable: Consistency Over Creativity

A mediocre post published on schedule will always outperform a perfect post that never goes live. Your enemy isn't bad content — it's no content. Post 5 times a week for 90 days straight and you will see results. The algorithm rewards consistency above all else. Don't overthink it. Use the templates, hit record, and ship.
