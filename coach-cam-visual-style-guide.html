The Lighting Rule
How RPN makes everything look cinematic — and how you will too
Video / Reels

Shoot with side lighting or backlight for depth. Avoid flat, overhead fluorescent lighting. Even a single ring light placed to the side creates dramatic shadows. Grade warm — push amber/gold in post. Slightly desaturate everything except skin tones.

Graphics / Carousels

Dark backgrounds (#0A0A0A or near-black). Text in white or gold. Never use bright or pastel backgrounds. Use subtle gradient overlays (dark-to-transparent) over photos. Add a slight vignette to corners of every image.

05 — CONTENT TIERS
Three Levels of Production
Not every post needs to be a short film. Here's how to balance effort with output.
01
Cinematic Signature Pieces
1-2x per week — your highest production content
What It Is

Mini-documentaries, cinematic Reels, high-production storytelling pieces. These are your RPN-inspired content — the posts people share and save. Title cards, cinematic lighting, dramatic editing, original music/sound design.

Production

Planned in advance. Script or shot list. Best lighting setup. Color graded. Custom thumbnail. Canva or Higgsfield for effects. Opus Clips for distribution cuts. Full caption with story arc. 60-90 seconds.

Examples

"THE SEPARATION — A Coach Cam Story" / "THE VETERAN'S EDGE" / "80+ EPISODES — The Origin" / "THE LEADERSHIP TEST" / Series premiere episodes

Goal

SHAREABILITY. These are the posts that go viral, get saved, and make people follow you. Optimized for Explore page and algorithmic distribution. Premium quality signals authority.

02
Story-Wrapped Education
2-3x per week — value content delivered through story
What It Is

Educational content wrapped in personal narrative. Instead of "5 tips for leadership," it's "What my drill sergeant taught me about delegation." The advice is the same — the delivery makes it unforgettable.

Production

Good lighting but not cinematic-level. Phone-quality video with clean audio is fine. Solid thumbnail (use Framework or Number templates). Carousels designed in Canva with dark theme. 30-60 seconds for Reels.

Examples

"The Tool That Changed My Coaching Business" / "Dad Mode: What My Kids Teach Me About Leadership" / "3 Lessons from 80+ Episodes" / Weekly AI tool reviews

Goal

TRUST & AUTHORITY. These posts demonstrate expertise while revealing who you are. They convert followers into fans. Optimized for saves and meaningful comments.

03
Raw & Real Moments
2-3x per week — unpolished, authentic, in-the-moment
What It Is

Phone-to-post content. Behind-the-scenes of building FLG, morning routines, quick takes on the drive, podcast setup clips, family moments (with permission). No editing required — the rawness IS the appeal.

Production

Selfie mode or phone propped up. Natural lighting. Record → trim → post. Stories and Reels format. No custom thumbnails needed for Stories. Quick text overlay in-app. Under 30 seconds.

Examples

"Building FLG: Week 1" daily updates / "4:30 AM — the CEO wake-up" / Quick reaction to industry news / Post-podcast recording debrief / "Just dropped the kids off, here's what I'm thinking"

Goal

CONNECTION & RELATABILITY. These posts make people feel like they know you personally. The contrast with your cinematic content makes BOTH formats more powerful. Optimized for DMs and Story engagement.

06 — STORY FRAMEWORK
The Coach Cam Story Structure
Based on your advisor's feedback: people want to know YOUR story. Here's how to tell it every time.
STEP 01

The Moment

Open with a specific moment in time. Not "I used to struggle with..." but "November 14, 2019. I'm sitting in my truck in the parking lot..."

→
STEP 02

The Tension

What was at stake? What could go wrong? "If this didn't work, I was going back to a life I'd already decided to leave."

→
STEP 03

The Insight

The lesson or realization. This is where your coaching expertise comes in. "That's when I realized leadership isn't about rank — it's about..."

→
STEP 04

The Bridge

Connect it to the viewer. "If you're sitting in YOUR truck right now, wondering if you should make the leap..."

→
STEP 05

The Invitation

Not a hard sell. An invitation into the journey. "Follow along — I'm building this in real time."

SPECIFIC MOMENT + REAL TENSION + LESSON + VIEWER BRIDGE

Every story follows this arc. The specificity is what makes it feel real. Vague stories don't connect.

10 — THE RULES
Do This / Not That
Quick reference for maintaining the Coach Cam blend. When in doubt, check here.

✓ DO THIS

  • Open every story with a specific moment in time
  • Use dark backgrounds on every thumbnail and carousel
  • Make Thursday your cinematic "event" day
  • Show the messy, in-progress parts of building FLG
  • Reference your military background naturally, not forcefully
  • Let family moments teach leadership lessons
  • Use gold/amber typography as your signature color
  • Create mystery with titles ("THE SEPARATION" not "My Story")
  • End with an invitation, not a pitch
  • Repurpose one podcast episode per week minimum
  • Post daily Stories even if they're 10-second check-ins
  • Use Higgsfield/Nano Banana for cinematic pieces only

✗ NOT THIS

  • Generic hooks like "5 things every entrepreneur needs"
  • Bright or pastel backgrounds on any visual content
  • Posting the same production quality every day (kills contrast)
  • Only showing wins — vulnerability builds trust faster
  • Leading with "As a veteran..." on every single post
  • Exploiting family moments without a real lesson attached
  • Inconsistent visual identity (random colors, fonts, vibes)
  • Clickbait that doesn't deliver on the promise
  • Hard selling coaching services in content captions
  • Letting 80+ episodes sit untouched — that's your goldmine
  • Going silent for days — consistency beats perfection
  • Over-editing raw content — rawness is the point of Tier 3
